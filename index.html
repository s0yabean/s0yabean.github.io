    Title: Uncovering Soccer Personas Using Clustering Algorithms¶

    Introduction (What Is Clustering?)¶

    In the realms of machine learning, a very common delineation is between:

    • supervised learning
    • unsupervised learning

    The key difference is whether a target variable of interest is available. In other words, is there something for the training data to predict and compare with?

    If not, it typically is an unsupervised learning problem, where we seek to unravel the "hidden" target variable using techniques that attempt to group similar data together.

    During past data science work, I have led and implemented 2 technical projects related to clustering in different industries, which has deepened my appreciation for this problem space.¶

    Usually, businesses would like to cluster commercial data for various purposes, such as discovering customer profiles to create mental models of their customers.

    The commercial work I did revolved around clustering consumers to rank them for targeted marketing campaigns and customer support, and the applications are plenty.

    However I wanted to switch it up and doing something fun while transferring my tacit knowledge, so I decided to work on soccer.

    I'm an avid soccer player - one of my biggest sporting achievements was winning the under 10 7-a-side National Championships in Singapore where I scored the winning goal.

    I never really reached that peak again even in my teens, but I've always grew strong domain expertise through futsal sessions and watching top European leagues - I supported Manchester United until Rooney left!

    Hence, I wanted to take this opportunity to cluster a bunch of player data and build a soccer player recommender using clustering techniques.¶
